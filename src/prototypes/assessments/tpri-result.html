Detailed Results for Abby

The Sharpen TPRI Reading Assessment identifies children who might need extra support with reading to be successful in their current grade. It is not intended to be an outcome assessment, and your child does not "pass" or "fail" this assessment. For the overall assessment result as well as for each sub-assessment, a child will get one of two results:

  • Developed (D): Your child was successful with the task, which means that they will likely make good progress with appropriate instruction; OR
  • Still developing (SD): Your child is still working to develop this skill, and may benefit from additional reading assistance and/or assessment.

Additionally:

  • N/A: The sub-assessment is not applicable at that timepoint
  • —: An expected sub-assessment was skipped or not yet completed

  • Letter Sounds (beginning of Kindergarten to middle of Grade 1)
    This sub-assessment relates to your child's ability to recognize letters and letter sounds. When these skills are undeveloped or developed late, it increases the likelihood of your child developing later reading problems.
  • Blending Phonemes (beginning of Kindergarten to middle of Grade 1)
    This sub-assessment relates to your child's ability to hear and play with the sounds or syllables in words. When these skills are undeveloped or developed late, it increases the likelihood of your child developing later reading problems.
  • Word Reading (beginning of Grade 1 to end of Grade 2)
    This sub-assessment relates to your child's ability to read a list of decodable words. When these skills are undeveloped or developed late, it increases the likelihood of your child developing later reading problems.
