Learners are placed in Level A when:

  • they still need to learn a number of letter-sound relationships
  • they need more instruction and practice to blend sounds to form words

(Even when a learner is able to get some right answers, there is still a need for repetition and growth to reach mastery.)

When learners have successfully completed Level A, they will be able to:

  • read about 90 common words such as mop and sat automatically
  • sound out almost any two- or three-letter word with the letter-sounds they have studied
  • easily read simple texts with these words

All learners will start with easier activities so they can get the hang of Sharpen Reading before the activities get harder.

More information

About Level A

Level A covers knowledge of the sounds for the one-letter consonants, the so-called "short" vowels, and the two-letter combinations ll, sh, ch, th, and ck. Students at this level learn how to break simple words into their individual sounds as well as to blend individual sounds to make a whole word, and to orally delete or substitute a single sound in a word.

About the Placement Assessment

This assessment evaluates the learner's current reading skills in order to determine the appropriate starting level in Sharpen Reading. It's important to note that the placement assessment helps identify the learner's current reading level and skills. This assessment is not a measure of the learner's current or future reading potential, but rather a tool to help create a learning plan that meets the learner's specific immediate needs.
